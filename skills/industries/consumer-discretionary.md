# Industry Skill: Consumer Discretionary

Covers: Retail (specialty, off-price, e-commerce), Restaurants, Autos & Parts, Leisure, Homebuilders, Luxury.

## KPIs to Extract and Monitor

**Retail**
- Same-store sales (SSS) / comparable sales growth — the primary revenue quality signal
- Inventory turns and weeks of supply (rising inventory > demand = markdown risk)
- Gross margin by channel (store vs. DTC vs. wholesale)
- E-commerce penetration and CAC trends
- Clearance/promotional cadence vs. prior year

**Restaurants**
- Comp traffic vs. comp ticket (ticket up + traffic down = consumer trading down)
- Restaurant-level EBITDA margin (target varies: QSR 25–30%, casual dining 15–20%)
- New unit economics: AUV, payback period
- Food/labour cost as % of restaurant revenue
- Franchise mix (asset-light = higher multiples)

**Autos / Auto Parts**
- SAAR (Seasonally Adjusted Annual Rate) for new vehicle sales
- Dealer inventory days (30–60 days = healthy; >80 days = incentive pressure)
- Used car price indices (Manheim)
- EV transition capex as % of revenue
- Parts: commercial vs. DIY mix, DIFM (do-it-for-me) share gains

**Homebuilders**
- Net order growth and cancellation rate (cancellation >20% = demand softening)
- Order backlog value and units
- Community count growth
- Lot cost and cycle time (build time)

## Valuation Frameworks
- Retailers: EV/EBITDA (6–12x), P/E, FCF yield
- Restaurants (franchise): EV/EBITDA (15–25x)
- Autos: P/E trough earnings, EV/EBITDA (4–8x), PEG
- Homebuilders: P/BV (1.0–2.0x tangible book), P/E on normalised earnings

## Key Risks
- **Consumer cycle sensitivity**: discretionary is first cut when real incomes decline
- **Inventory bloat**: post-2021 overorder cycle taught hard lessons on markdown risk
- **Private label share gains**: Costco / Amazon Basics compressing branded margins
- **Interest rate sensitivity**: homebuilders and big-ticket autos directly rate-driven
- **Labour costs**: minimum wage hikes hit QSR/casual dining margins structurally

## Sector-Specific Flags
- SSS < -3% for two consecutive quarters — structural share loss vs. cyclical
- Inventory growth outpacing revenue growth for 2+ quarters
- COGS step-up while revenue is flat — input cost pass-through failure
- High lease leverage (operating lease / EBITDA > 4x) in retail = balance sheet risk

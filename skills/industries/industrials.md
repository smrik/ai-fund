# Industry Skill: Industrials

Covers: Aerospace & Defense, Machinery (general, agricultural, construction), Transportation (rail, trucking, logistics), Electrical Equipment, Building Products.

## KPIs to Extract and Monitor

**Aerospace & Defense**
- Book-to-bill ratio for commercial aerospace (Boeing/Airbus backlog = 7–8 years)
- Defense budget trajectory and programme funding in Congress appropriations
- Aftermarket vs. OEM revenue mix (aftermarket = high margin, recurring)
- Narrowbody vs. widebody production rate ramp (737 MAX, A320neo line rates)
- Supply chain bottlenecks: aerostructures, engines, avionics

**Machinery**
- PMI (Manufacturing Purchasing Managers Index) — leading indicator for orders
- End-market exposure: construction, agriculture, mining, energy
- Short-cycle (book-and-ship) vs. long-cycle (project) revenue mix
- Price realisation vs. raw material (steel, copper) cost
- Dealer inventory and channel fill

**Transportation / Logistics**
- Load-to-truck ratio (spot freight) — Cass Freight Index, DAT
- Rail volumes by commodity: intermodal, automotive, coal, grain
- Operating ratio (OR) — trucking target <85%; rail target <60%
- Fuel surcharge mechanism — does it fully offset fuel volatility?
- E-commerce parcel volumes (FedEx/UPS)

**Electrical Equipment**
- Utility capex cycle (grid modernisation, EV charging, data centre power)
- Order backlog and lead times
- Reshoring / nearshoring as incremental demand driver

## Valuation Frameworks
- Aerospace/Defense: EV/EBITDA (10–16x), P/E, FCF yield
- Machinery: P/E on mid-cycle earnings, EV/EBITDA (8–13x)
- Transport: EV/EBITDA (8–12x), operating ratio trends, P/E
- Electrical: EV/EBITDA (12–20x for secular growth names)

## What to Search For in News
- "ISM PMI manufacturing [month]"
- "freight rates trucking spot market [month]"
- "[Ticker] backlog orders book-to-bill [quarter]"
- "defense budget authorization [year]"
- "Boeing Airbus production rate [quarter]"

## Key Risks
- **PMI cycle**: manufacturing recession compresses orders rapidly; mean-reversion underestimated
- **Commodity cost timing**: steel/copper spike hits machinery margins 1–2 quarters before pricing catches up
- **Defense sequestration or continuing resolution**: programme delays hit revenue recognition
- **Aviation OEM production ramp risk**: Boeing 737 MAX production rate target repeatedly missed
- **Labour disputes**: UAW/IAM strikes in aerospace/auto supply chain

## Sector-Specific Flags
- Backlog cancellations rising as % of new orders — demand signal deteriorating
- Gross margin compression in machinery while peers hold — company-specific execution issue
- Pension liability large relative to market cap — hidden balance sheet risk (aerospace legacy)
- Progress billing reversal or cost overrun on long-term contracts — revenue recognition risk

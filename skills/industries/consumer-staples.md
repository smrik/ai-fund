# Industry Skill: Consumer Staples

Covers: Food & Beverage (packaged, branded), Household Products, Personal Care, Tobacco, Food Retail/Distribution.

## KPIs to Extract and Monitor

**Branded CPG (Consumer Packaged Goods)**
- Organic revenue growth split: price vs. volume — volume growth confirms demand; price-only = unsustainable
- Market share by category (IRI/Nielsen scan data)
- Gross margin trend — input cost pass-through lag typically 1–2 quarters
- A&P (advertising & promotion) spend as % of sales — cutting A&P props short-term margins, destroys brand
- Innovation pipeline and new SKU contribution to growth

**Food & Beverage**
- Category volumes (IRI/Nielsen retail scanner)
- Private label encroachment — premium brand vulnerability
- Raw material exposure: grain, edible oils, packaging, dairy, sugar, aluminium
- DTC / e-commerce penetration
- International emerging market volumes (growth engine for large-cap staples)

**Tobacco**
- Combustible volume decline rate (structural -3% to -5%/yr)
- NGP (next-gen product) net revenue share: vaping, heated tobacco, oral nicotine
- Price elasticity — can pricing offset volume decline?
- Regulatory risk: FDA PMTA, menthol ban, nicotine caps

**Food Retail / Distribution**
- Same-store sales and basket size
- Private label mix (margin accretive)
- Shrink rate (theft/waste)
- Fuel surcharge and logistics cost as % of COGS

## Valuation Frameworks
- Large-cap CPG: EV/EBITDA (13–18x), DCF, P/E (20–28x for quality compounder)
- Tobacco: P/E (10–14x, dividend-adjusted), EV/EBITDA (8–12x), FCF yield
- Food retail: EV/EBITDA (6–10x), P/E, FCF yield

## Key Risks
- **Destocking cycle**: trade inventory drawdown can cause 2–3 quarter revenue air pocket
- **Private label substitution accelerating**: post-inflation consumer trade-down may be permanent
- **Input cost timing mismatch**: hedges roll off at higher costs
- **Valuation premium at risk**: staples re-rate down sharply when bonds offer real yield competition
- **GLP-1 obesity drugs**: structural volume risk for high-calorie food and snack categories

## Sector-Specific Flags
- Volume declines >2% with strong pricing — consumer pushback; share loss
- A&P as % of revenue declining for 2+ years — brand milking
- DIO rising in food — shelf-life / perishables inventory risk
- Accounts payable extension beyond 90 days in food distribution — supplier financing stress

# Industry Skill: Communication Services

Covers: Telecom (wireless, wireline, cable), Media (streaming, broadcast, publishing), Advertising Platforms (social, search, display), Gaming.

## KPIs to Extract and Monitor

**Telecom (Wireless/Cable)**
- Subscriber net adds by service (postpaid phone, broadband, video)
- ARPU (average revenue per user) — postpaid phone ARPU trend
- Churn rate: postpaid phone churn <1% is healthy; rising churn = competitive pressure
- EBITDA margin and capex intensity (capex as % of revenue)
- Free cash flow after capex — the real shareholder return metric
- Network quality scores (Ookla, RootMetrics, J.D. Power)
- Fibre penetration rate in footprint

**Streaming / Media**
- Subscriber or MAU/DAU growth
- ARPU and average revenue per subscriber
- Content cost amortisation and cash content spend vs. P&L
- Engagement metrics (hours per user, retention)
- Ad-supported tier penetration and CPM trends

**Advertising Platforms**
- Revenue per user / ARPU
- Impressions served and CPM/CPC pricing
- Time spent / engagement as leading indicator
- Apple ATT privacy impact on ad targeting (iOS)
- AI-driven ad product roadmap (search generative experience)

**Gaming**
- Live-service revenue (recurring vs. front-loaded)
- Daily active users and ARPDAU
- Pipeline of new releases and franchise cadence

## Valuation Frameworks
- Telecom: EV/EBITDA (6–9x), FCF yield, regulated asset value
- Streaming/Media: EV/EBITDA (8–15x), subscriber economics NPV, FCF yield
- Ad platforms: EV/EBITDA (12–25x), EV/Revenue for high-growth, FCF yield
- Gaming: EV/EBITDA (10–18x), EV/Revenue

## Key Risks
- **Cord-cutting acceleration**: linear TV bundle unravelling faster than streaming replaces it
- **Advertising cyclicality**: digital ad revenue highly correlated with macro (dropped 20% in 2022)
- **Fibre overbuild competition**: new entrant fibre (Frontier, BEAD programme) in telco footprints
- **Content cost inflation**: streaming bidding wars for sports rights and original content
- **Regulatory / antitrust**: Section 230, EU DMA, proposed ad-platform breakups

## Sector-Specific Flags
- Postpaid phone churn rising sequentially — price increase pushback or competitive loss
- Telecom capex guidance raised mid-year without backlog increase — capital discipline concern
- Platform daily active user growth below monthly (engagement hollowing)
- Content amortisation period extended — accounting change that flatters near-term margins
- Ad platform engagement metrics disclosed less frequently — potential deterioration hiding

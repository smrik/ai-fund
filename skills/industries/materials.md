# Industry Skill: Materials

Covers: Chemicals (specialty, commodity), Metals & Mining (steel, copper, aluminium, gold, lithium), Paper & Packaging, Construction Materials (cement, aggregates).

## KPIs to Extract and Monitor

**Specialty Chemicals**
- Volume growth by end-market (auto, semis, consumer, ag)
- Price/volume split — price-driven growth less durable
- Raw material spread: key feedstocks (ethylene, propylene, methanol)
- EBITDA margin by segment
- Working capital: chemical companies build large inventory buffers

**Commodity Chemicals**
- Capacity utilisation industry-wide (pricing power above 85%)
- Global ethylene / polyethylene / TDI / MDI price curves
- China export pressure — excess capacity dumping
- Integrated vs. merchant buyer spread economics

**Metals & Mining**
- Unit cost of production (C1 cost for copper in $/lb, AISC for gold in $/oz)
- Ore grade trend — declining grades = higher cost over time
- LME / COMEX spot price vs. company hedging
- Mine plan and reserve life (years)
- Capex: sustaining vs. growth; stay-in-business cost

**Construction Materials**
- Aggregates pricing per ton — highly local, pricing power test
- Cement volumes and utilisation (typically 85%+ = pricing power)
- Housing starts and infrastructure spending as demand drivers
- Energy cost (kiln fuel = cement key cost driver)

## Valuation Frameworks
- Specialty chemicals: EV/EBITDA (8–15x), through-cycle P/E
- Commodity chemicals: mid-cycle EV/EBITDA (5–8x), EV/capacity
- Mining: EV/EBITDA (4–8x), NAV-based, P/NAV, NPV per oz/lb
- Construction materials: EV/EBITDA (10–16x), FCF yield

## Key Risks
- **China demand slowdown**: 50–60% of global copper/aluminium/steel demand is China
- **Capacity oversupply**: Chinese commodity chemical exports flooding Western markets
- **Energy cost sensitivity**: smelting (aluminium), cement kilns, chemical crackers
- **Currency risk**: mining companies with USD costs but local-currency revenues
- **ESG/regulatory**: tailings dam failures, carbon border adjustment mechanisms (CBAM)

## Sector-Specific Flags
- EBITDA margin compression while peers hold — company-specific cost or pricing failure
- Capex materially above D&A for 3+ years without production growth — capital efficiency issue
- Impairment of mine assets — goodwill write-down on acquisitions at commodity cycle peak
- Accounts receivable spike in specialty chemicals — credit quality of downstream customers
